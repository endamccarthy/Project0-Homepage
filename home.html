Summary

Currently up-skilling in computer science having spent 4 years working as an electrical engineer with Arup Consulting Engineers.

I left Arup in December 2018 to pursue a career change. I am currently self studying and will enrol in a full time software development course in September 2019.

I was employed by Arup in the Cork office after my graduation in 2014. I spent just over 3 years in Cork (including a 3 month assignment in the Singapore office) before attaining a long-term assignment to the Dubai office where I spent just over 1 year.

I have very high standards in terms of quality. Reviewing and checking work has always been a high priority for me. One of my strongest skillsets is my ability to learn and adapt to new software programs and technologies.

Career Experience


Electrical Engineer, Arup Dubai (Oct 2017 – Dec 2018)

Much of my time in Dubai was spent in the role of project engineer on site in Abu Dhabi. I also worked on various other projects ranging from detailed designs to peer reviews.

Some of my duties included:

  • Resolving various design issues on site and carrying out daily site inspections.
  • Responding to contractor submittals, i.e. RFI's, material approvals, shop drawings, etc.
  • Attending progress meetings involving the client, contractor and project management.
  • Developing detailed electrical designs using the Revit software program (4D building information modelling software).
  • Preparing reports and presentations.
  • Carrying out peer reviews of electrical systems on final detailed designs.


Electrical Design Engineer, Arup Cork (Aug 2014 – Oct 2017)

The majority of these 3 years was spent working on the detailed electrical design of several metro stations located in the Middle East. I also worked on various other detailed design projects including a confidential hospital project.
I gained an in-depth knowledge of the use of Revit throughout this time. I also became familiar with a visual programming software called Dynamo BIM, this software enabled us to access the Revit API and create scripts (using Python) to automate any number of processes in Revit.

My duties throughout the 3 years in Cork included developing:

  • Fire alarm designs including loop schedules and schematics.
  • Small power designs including Amtech calculations.
  • Normal and emergency lighting designs including DIALux calculations. 
  • Electrical schematics and maximum demand calculations.


Apprentice Electrician, ESB Networks, Ireland (Sep 2009 – Sep 2011)

I joined ESB Networks in 2009 as an apprentice electrician after finishing secondary school. There was a programme in place at the time which would see a select few apprentices receive scholarships to pursue an engineering degree at some point during their apprenticeship. I successfully obtained an ESB scholarship and was enrolled in 2nd year of electrical engineering in Cork IT in September 2011.

Some of my duties included the construction and maintenance of HV, MV and LV networks (both overhead and underground), panel wiring in sub-stations, new customer connections, urban network renewal and storm damage repair.
